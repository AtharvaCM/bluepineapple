Camelids

The four llamas and two camels are camelids. Camelids tend to be large and are strictly herbivorous. Camelids differ from ruminants in several ways. They have three-chambered stomachs, rather than four-chambered ones. Their upper lips are split in two, with each part separately mobile; and an isolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body. look longer still. (from Wikipedia)
